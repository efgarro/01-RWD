José María Figueres Ferrer

"Don Pepe"

25 September 1906 - 8 June 1990

  • Don Pepe served as president of Costa Rica on three occasions; 1948-1949, 1953-1958, and 1970-1974.
  • The signature acomplishments of his first term in office were; abolition of the army, nationalization of the the banking system, and voting rights for women and Afro-costarican citizens.
  • Between 1924 and 1928 he worked and studied in Boston at the Massachussets Institute of Technology. At MIT he studied Hydroelectrical Engineering.
  • Upon his return to Costa Rica, he bought a farm in coffee rich region of Tarrazú, where with almost profetic forsight, he named the estate “La Lucha sin Fín“ (The Endless Struggle)
  • His criticism of the government of Rafael Angel Calderón Guardia in July 1942 forced him into exile in Mexico for two years.
  • Figueres led an uprising in support of Otilio Ulate, whom in 1948, legitimaly won the presidential election. The then president, Rafael A. Calderón Guardia, and the Legislature annulled the election.
  • The two-month civil war ended when Calderón’s forces, despite being backed by Nicaraguan strongman Anastasio Somoza, capitulated.
  • He described the uprising of 1948 as a “revolution of the middle class.“
  • Elected president by a landslide in 1953, Figueres pledged his government would follow a “pro-United States“ policy.
  • Figueres was a firmly anticommunist social democrat, and during this period he instituted many social and economic reforms.
  • Figueres also opposed the dictatorial regime in pre-Castro Cuba and went so far as to dispatch a planeload of weapons for Cuban insurgents led by the young Fidel Castro
  • Figueres was well liked and received in many Latin American countries for his center-left ideals. He has been called one of the greatest contributors to the social democratic ideology.
